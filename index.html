The newsletter

An operator who already grows for free

Grow My Newsletter is a weekly publication read by ~13,000 newsletter operators, teaching them how to grow and monetize. Its founder runs one of the sharpest paid-growth engines in the space. He acquires subscribers on Meta, then uses SparkLoop paid recommendations and brand offers to recover the full cost of acquisition.

The math: he spends about $2.50 to acquire a subscriber and makes back about $2.60 through SparkLoop, so the list grows, effectively, for free. Growth was never the problem. Knowing who those subscribers were, and turning them into revenue, was.

The challenge

You can't monetize a subscriber you can't see

Every month, hundreds of new subscribers joined, and most stayed anonymous. The old welcome survey, built first on Typeform and later a beehiiv tool, topped out at ~30% completion. So 70% of the subscribers he'd paid to acquire told him nothing about themselves.

Worse, learning anything useful meant stacking on more questions. Role, company size, location. That only dragged completion lower. And spotting which subscribers might actually sponsor the newsletter was manual, slow, and mostly never happened.

"I don't need to do any of those boring nonsense questions, because SparkLoop just has this AI enrichment built in."

Louis, Grow My Newsletter
The solution

A welcome survey that grows and monetizes the list

He switched his welcome flow to SparkLoop Welcome Surveys. Three things changed at once.

1. Twice as many subscribers became known. The survey opens a "complete your free subscription" loop and previews the welcome offers a subscriber unlocks by finishing. Completion jumped from 30% to 65%+. More than double the audience intelligence, from the same traffic.

2. AI enrichment replaced the boring questions. Role, company size, and location now arrive automatically with every submission. He asks 3 or 4 quick questions, and SparkLoop fills in the rest.

3. Sponsors surface themselves. SparkLoop enriches the whole list and automatically flags the 1 to 2% of new subscribers who are a strong fit to sponsor the newsletter. Leads he'd never have found by hand.
